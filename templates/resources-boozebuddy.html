Alcohol and Addiction Awareness Resources

Welcome to our dedicated resource page for spreading awareness about alcoholism and addiction. Our goal is to provide comprehensive information and learning resources to help individuals, families, and communities understand the impact of alcoholism and addiction. Below, you will find a collection of statistics, awareness campaigns, and educational materials designed to inform and support those affected by these issues.

Alcoholism and Addiction Statistics

Global Impact of Alcoholism: It's estimated that globally, around 1 percent of the population has an alcohol use disorder. Alcohol use disorders are responsible for 95,510 deaths in the U.S. each year.
‍
Addiction in the United States: 40 million Americans ages 12 and older have substance problems, with 75% of all high school students having used addictive substances.
‍
Youth and Alcohol: 1 in 4 Americans who began using any addictive substance before age 18 has a substance problem, compared to 1 in 25 Americans who started using at age 21 or older.
‍

Awareness Campaigns

"Talk. They Hear You." Campaign:
Aims to reduce underage drinking and other substance use among youths under the age of 21 by providing parents and caregivers with the resources they need to address the issue of alcohol and other drugs with children
‍
"Drink Less, Be Your Best" Campaign:
 Encourages US adults who drink excessively to drink less to improve their quality of life, relationships, and health

Departing Notes

This resource page is part of our commitment to raising awareness and providing support around the issues of alcoholism and addiction. If you or someone you know is struggling with alcohol or substance use, we encourage you to seek help. Remember, recovery is possible, and support is available.For more information, visit the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) website or call their National Helpline at 1-800-662-HELP (4357).

The information provided on this page is for educational purposes only and is not intended as medical advice. If you are dealing with a medical emergency, please contact your healthcare provider or emergency services immediately.
